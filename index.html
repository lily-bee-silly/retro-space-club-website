WELCOME! ホームへようこそ

Hello, fellow stargazers! You have reached the official home page of the Astronomy Club @ Needham High School. We meet every week to look up, talk shop, and figure out what that weird bright dot over the football field actually is (usually Jupiter).

Whether you've never used a telescope before or you can recite the Messier catalog from memory, there's a spot for you here. Grab your star chart and come say hi!

  • Next meeting: Thursday, Room 214, 3:00 PMNEW
  • Telescope training night signups now open
  • Perseid meteor shower watch party — details in Events

ABOUT THE CLUB 部について

Founded in 1987, the Astronomy Club is Needham High's longest-running science club. We're a low-pressure, high-curiosity group — no prior experience or equipment required. The club owns two Dobsonian reflectors and a solar filter, all available for member use at meetings and star parties.

ASTRONOMY NIGHTS 天体観測の夜

Every other Friday, we set up telescopes in the Newman Elementary School parking lot for a community stargazing night — clear skies permitting!

  • Every other Friday, 7:00 – 9:00 PMBIWEEKLY
  • Newman Elementary School parking lot
  • Free hot cocoa ☕ served all night
  • Club members on hand with telescopes pointed at the Moon & visible planets
  • All ages welcome — bring the whole family!

Got questions about what you're looking at? That's the whole point — our members love explaining what's in the eyepiece, from craters to Saturn's rings.

OUR TELESCOPES 望遠鏡について

The club owns and maintains a small fleet of telescopes, all available for members to check out at meetings and Astronomy Nights. No experience needed — an officer will walk you through setup.

TelescopeTypeBest For
"Big Blue"10" DobsonianDeep-sky objects, nebulae, star clusters
"Little Blue"8" DobsonianMoon, planets, easy portability
"The Traveler"80mm RefractorOff-site trips & beginner training
Solar Filter KitFits any club scopeDaytime sunspot viewing

⚠️ Never look at the Sun without a proper solar filter! Ask an officer before any daytime observing.
